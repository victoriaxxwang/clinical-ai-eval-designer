## 1. Study Design

Frame outcome as time-to-event (5-year incident T2DM) with explicit censoring, not a static binary, because "5-year risk" is inherently survival data.
**Rationale:** The retrieved literature is almost entirely single-cohort cross-sectional *diagnosis/prediction* modeling (LightGBM diagnosis, PMID 34574055; ML predictive models, PMID 31615566; ensemble Type-2 prediction, OpenAlex W4388982729; gradient-boosted tree diagnosis, OpenAlex W3038710713; review PMID 28138367). ## 4. Ground Truth Strategy
**Recommendation:** Define incident T2DM by a pre-specified composite reference standard applied over the 5-year window: ≥2 lab-confirmed criteria (HbA1c ≥6.5% or FPG ≥126 mg/dL, per standard diagnostic thresholds) and/or a new diabetes diagnosis code plus glucose-lowering prescription, with a documented adjudication rule for discordant signals and explicit handling of prediabetes-to-diabetes transitions. Pre-specify censoring for loss-to-follow-up, death, and type-1/secondary diabetes exclusion. Quantify and report **reference-standard noise**: screening-detected vs. clinically-diagnosed cases differ in ascertainment, and undiagnosed-but-prevalent cases misclassified as "incident" cap achievable performance.
**Rationale:** The retrieved diagnosis-oriented papers rely on registry/dataset labels (e.g., PMID 34574055, PMID 31615566) whose ascertainment method is often opaque — a documented, adjudicated, lab-anchored outcome is the upgrade required for a prognostic deployment. Because incident-disease labels depend on whether patients were actually tested during follow-up (ascertainment bias), label reliability is a hard ceiling on measured sensitivity/specificity and must be stated as such.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — diagnostic criteria are standard; ascertainment/adjudication design needs expert specification.

**DOES NOT CERTIFY:**

- That risk estimates hold under **assay platforms, populations, or EHR sources not represented** in the validation cohorts.
- **Physiological ground truth** where the HbA1c/glucose reference standard is itself confounded (anemia, hemoglobinopathy, CKD, ascertainment bias) — achievable performance is capped by label reliability.
