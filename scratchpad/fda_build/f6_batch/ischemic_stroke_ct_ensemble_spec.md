## 1. Study Design
**Recommendation:** Two-stage design. **Stage A (standalone algorithm performance + multi-reader multi-case (MRMC) reader study):** retrospective, multi-center, consecutive-enrollment cohort of adults imaged with CT/CTP for suspected LVO within 24 h. Compare (i) standalone segmentation/mismatch output against the reference standard, and (ii) clinician triage decisions with vs. without the device (MRMC, crossover reader design), since the inferred claim is *assist*, not autonomous. **Stage B (prospective silent-mode / real-world):** deploy read-only in the ED/neuro-IR workflow to measure agreement and failure modes on live acquisitions before any interpretive influence. Pre-register endpoints, imaging inclusion criteria, and the mismatch-ratio threshold BEFORE analysis.
**Rationale:** Retrieved QAS clearances for directly analogous CT stroke triage software — Brainomix 360 Triage Stroke (K251983, K232496), Methinks CTA Stroke (K251590), Methinks NCCT Stroke (K250685), Rapid NCCT Stroke (K222884) — establish that this device class is cleared as notification/triage/quantification software, which favors a standalone-performance + reader/workflow design rather than a randomized outcome trial. The clinical evidence base for mismatch-guided thrombectomy triage itself derives from extended-window trials (DEFUSE-3/DAWN-type designs) not retrieved here — the team should confirm those RCTs to anchor the clinical rationale, but this device does not need to re-run an outcome RCT to support an assist/quantification claim. Segmentation-method literature (PMID 42187401; PMID 41920480; PMID 41975201; PMID 42280845 nnU-Net deployment) supports the technical approach but not clinical validity. No matching NCT trial was retrieved for this specific model.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — pathway analogues are strong; the exact design (MRMC parameters, silent-mode duration) is a judgment call.

## 2. Sensor / Input Validation (Pre-Condition)
**Recommendation:** Treat CTP acquisition and perfusion-map generation as the gating pre-condition that must pass BEFORE any segmentation/mismatch claim is credible. Qualify: (a) CT scanner make/model/detector rows and reconstruction kernel; (b) the CTP post-processing engine that produces CBF/CBV/Tmax/MTT — deconvolution algorithm, arterial-input-function selection, and vendor (these maps are software-derived and are NOT vendor-interchangeable); (c) contrast bolus timing/injection-rate adequacy and truncation; (d) patient-motion and beam-hardening artifact rejection; (e) co-registration accuracy between NCCT and CTP channels and left/right symmetry alignment (the contralateral-subtraction branch fails silently if midline registration is off). Establish an automatic input-quality gate that rejects or flags non-diagnostic studies rather than segmenting them.
**Rationale:** The mismatch ratio is only as valid as the Tmax/CBF maps feeding it, and perfusion thresholds are known to differ across post-processing platforms — a physics/software threat, not a footnote. The symmetry-aware branch introduces a specific new failure mode: contralateral pathology (old infarct, prior surgery) or mis-registration corrupts the subtraction. The Philips DigitalDiagnost recall (Z-0272-2024) and emergency-device MAUDE malfunction reports in the retrieved set are reminders that acquisition hardware/software fails in the field; input-validity monitoring is mandatory. No retrieved record quantifies cross-vendor perfusion-map agreement for this model — this must be measured.
**Confidence:** HIGH — that input validity gates downstream validity is well-established for perfusion imaging; the specific thresholds are unmeasured.

## 3. Performance Benchmarks
**Recommendation:** Pre-specify a tiered endpoint set: (1) **segmentation agreement** — voxelwise Dice/overlap and, more clinically, volumetric agreement (Bland–Altman bias + limits of agreement) for core volume and penumbra volume; (2) **mismatch-ratio agreement** — continuous agreement and, critically, **binary classification accuracy** (sensitivity/specificity) for the thrombectomy-relevant mismatch threshold vs. reference; (3) **per-territory/ASPECTS-region accuracy**; (4) **reader-study effect** — change in clinician triage accuracy/time with vs. without device. **No FDA-binding numeric benchmark was retrieved** for any of these — QAS clearances (K251983, K250685, K222884, etc.) are cleared on time-to-notification and standalone agreement metrics whose specific values are not in the retrieved records. Therefore the target values must be **set by the study team / expert panel a priori**, ideally benchmarked against published performance of the cleared predicates (obtain their 510(k) summaries).
**Rationale:** Literature (PMID 42187401; PMID 41920480; PMID 41975201) reports Dice-type figures for stroke segmentation, but these are research metrics on curated datasets and are NOT regulatory thresholds — do not import them as targets. The clinically decisive metric is mismatch-class agreement at the decision threshold, not mean Dice.

## 4. Ground Truth Strategy
**Recommendation:** Use a composite, imperfect reference. **Ischemic core:** follow-up imaging final infarct (DWI-MRI at 24–72 h, or follow-up NCCT/CTP) co-registered to the acute study, adjudicated by ≥2 blinded neuroradiologists with tie-breaker — recognizing reperfusion status confounds "final infarct = acute core." **Penumbra:** there is **no clean gold standard**; the operational reference is expert-defined perfusion thresholds or tissue-fate modeling, which is itself model-dependent and noisy. Record reperfusion (recanalization/TICI) status as a covariate because it determines whether at-risk tissue was salvaged or infarcted.
**Rationale:** Label reliability is a ceiling: because penumbra has no biological gold standard and core reference depends on reperfusion outcome, achievable sensitivity/specificity is capped by reference-standard noise — this must be stated in the study report and factored into the powering. Multi-rater adjudication with reported inter-rater agreement is required to characterize that ceiling. Retrieved segmentation papers largely use expert manual masks or challenge labels (e.g., BraTS-type curation, W2900298334), which carry the same limitation.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — the follow-up-infarct approach is standard; penumbra reference remains fundamentally contested.

## What This Validation Certifies — and What It Does Not
**CERTIFIES:** That, on emergency CT/CTP studies acquired on the qualified scanners and post-processing platforms in adults imaged within 24 h for suspected LVO, the device's automated core/penumbra volumes and mismatch ratio agree with a defined imperfect reference standard within pre-specified limits — including in each pre-specified subgroup — and that trained clinicians using it as an *assist* triage the pre-specified thrombectomy-relevant mismatch category at a measured accuracy.
**DOES NOT CERTIFY:**
- That the segmented penumbra reflects true tissue fate — there is no biological gold standard; accuracy is capped by reference-standard noise.
- That using the device improves patient outcomes (functional independence, mortality) — no outcome/efficacy claim is supported without an outcome trial.
- Autonomous or standalone thrombectomy eligibility determination — the certified claim is clinician-assist only.
- Performance on scanners, CTP post-processing versions, or populations outside those studied, or on studies failing the input-quality gate.
- Regulatory clearance — passing this study is evidence for a submission, not clearance itself.
- Validity where contralateral-hemisphere pathology or mis-registration corrupts the symmetry-subtraction branch, unless explicitly tested.
